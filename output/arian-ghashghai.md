so @feltsense just did something kinda nuts: they took 20 startups from the YC W26 batch and replicated them using agentic AI founders. full products, live, in days.

and look, i'm not here to dunk on YC. YC produces incredible founders. that's not the point. the point is that the thing VCs have been pattern-matching on for years (i.e. "can this team ship fast") is getting stress-tested in a way nobody's comfortable talking about yet.

speed-to-market used to be the moat. or at least that's what everyone told themselves. build fast, raise fast, grow fast, figure out defensibility later. but if an AI agent can replicate your core product in 72 hours, then "we shipped it first" is not a strategy. it's a timestamp.

what's actually interesting here: some of these startups couldn't be replicated. that's the signal. the ones where the product is downstream of something harder to copy (proprietary data, deep domain insight, network effects that compound) those are the ones worth paying attention to.

the uncomfortable question for VCs and founders alike: if you strip away the pedigree, the batch, the demo day momentum, what's actually left? because agents don't care about your cap table or your YC badge. they just build.

imo this isn't a threat to good founders. it's a mirror. and most of the industry is not ready to look.
